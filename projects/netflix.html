Financial Analysis · January 2025 – May 2025

Equity Analysis – Netflix (NFLX)

A comprehensive three-year financial statement analysis of Netflix evaluating profitability, liquidity, leverage, and cash flow performance from 2022 to 2024, with strategic recommendations for investors and management.

Overview

The Analysis

This project analyzed three years of Netflix's publicly available financial statements to evaluate the company's financial health, profitability trajectory, and long-term sustainability. The goal was to produce a structured equity analysis similar to what a financial analyst would prepare for investor reporting or internal strategy review.

Income Statement
Profitability, margins, EPS trends
Balance Sheet
Assets, liabilities, equity structure
Cash Flow
Operating, investing, free cash flow
Key Findings

Free cash flow expansion from 2022 to 2024

The analysis also evaluated liquidity ratios (current ratio improved from 1.17 to 1.22), leverage (debt-to-equity declined from 1.34 to 1.17 indicating improving balance sheet health), and cash flow margins (from 5.1% to 18.9%).

Recommendations

Strategic Insights

Based on the financial analysis, key recommendations were presented around debt management given Netflix's continued reliance on debt financing, revenue diversification through advertising and live content strategies, and sustaining margin improvements while investing in content production.

The analysis was presented to class stakeholders using structured financial dashboards built in Tableau, translating complex ratio analysis into a clear business narrative.
